\subsection{Physical motivation for non-minimal coupling}

Wormhole throats supported by~\eqref{eq:profiles} necessarily involve regions of large curvature and, for the reference parameters~\eqref{eq:prd-params}, an equatorial ergoregion in which frame dragging dominates the stationary Killing norm~\eqref{eq:gtt}.
In such backgrounds, a minimally coupled quantum scalar field is a severe idealization: curvature-induced effective masses, higher-curvature corrections, and finite-resolution effects all introduce intrinsic microscopic scales~\cite{BirrellDavies1982,Sotiriou2010}.
A minimal model that retains a single additional coupling is the non-minimal scalar-tensor interaction $\zeta\,\phif R$.
Dimensionally, $\zeta\,\phif$ carries units of length squared (in geometric units), so the combination
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:l-star-heuristic}
\ell_{\star}^{2}\sim \frac{\hbar\,|\zeta\,\phif|}{|R|}
\end{equation}
defines a curvature-referenced cutoff length.
Near $\Sigma_{\mathrm{ERGO}}$, where light-cone tipping is strongest and curvature invariants remain finite but non-negligible for the present profiles, $\ell_{\star}$ provides a natural ultraviolet floor beneath which a local effective description of $\langle\Tmn\rangle_{\mathrm{ren}}$ should not be trusted without additional degrees of freedom.

We emphasize the logical status of this construction:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\zeta\,\phif R$ is an EFT deformation of the field sector, not a dynamical mechanism that creates or destroys an ergoregion;
  \item $\ell_{\star}$ is a modeling scale inferred from background curvature, not an output of the Rust monodromy engine;
  \item the resulting estimate is a qualitative regulator near $\Sigma_{\mathrm{ERGO}}$; it does not assert chronology protection, continuum QFT theorems, or a numerical evaluation of $\langle\Tmn\rangle_{\mathrm{ren}}$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Hadamard parametrix in four dimensions}

As an analytic outlook, consider the renormalized two-point function for a
non-minimally coupled scalar field with Hadamard expansion
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:hadamard}
G^{(1)}(x,x')
= \frac{1}{8\pi^{2}}
\Bigl[
\frac{u(x,x')}{\sigma_{\epsilon}(x,x')}
+ v(x,x')\ln\!\Bigl(\frac{\sigma_{\epsilon}(x,x')}{\mu^{2}}\Bigr)
+ w(x,x')
\Bigr],
\end{equation}
where $\sigma(x,x')$ is the Synge world function (half the squared geodesic
interval), $\sigma_{\epsilon}=\sigma-i\epsilon$ regulates the coincidence
limit, and $u,v,w$ are smooth biscalar coefficients determined by the field
equation~\cite{DeWitt1975,BirrellDavies1982}. Near coincidence,
$\sigma=\tfrac12\,g_{\mu\nu}\xi^{\mu}\xi^{\nu}+O(|\xi|^{3})$ in Riemann
normal coordinates $\xi^{\mu}$.

For the non-minimal scalar-tensor coupling
$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ST}}=\tfrac12(\partial\phif)^{2}+\zeta\,\phif R$,
variation yields the modified Klein--Gordon equation
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:modified-kg}
\bigl(\Box - m^{2} - \zeta R\bigr)\,\phif = 0,
\end{equation}
equivalent to a minimally coupled field with curvature-induced effective mass
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:m-eff}
m_{\mathrm{eff}}^{2}(x)=m^{2}+\zeta R(x)
\end{equation}
(distinct from the azimuthal mode mass $m_{\ast}$ of the discrete monodromy diagnostics).
The Hadamard coefficient $u(x,x')$ acquires a curvature-dependent correction
$u\to u[1+\tfrac16\zeta R\,\sigma+O(\sigma^{2})]$, and the coincidence
pole $1/\sigma_{\epsilon}$ is \emph{proposed} to be regulated by the intrinsic
length scale
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:l-star-full}
\ell_{\star}
=\Bigl(\frac{\hbar\,|\zeta\,\phif|}{|R|_{\Sigma_{\mathrm{ERGO}}}}\Bigr)^{\!1/2},
\end{equation}
obtained by balancing the $\sigma^{-1}$ term against the
curvature-induced mass in~\eqref{eq:m-eff} on the ergosurface.
This cutoff is an EFT modeling assumption, not a quantity computed by the
present numerical engine.

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{Null-winding truncation and a proposed bound on $\langle T_{\mu\nu}\rangle_{\mathrm{ren}}$}{Null-winding truncation and a proposed bound on renormalized stress-energy}}

In the chronology-horizon literature, null geodesics winding $n$ times about a
causality boundary with proper-length scales $\Delta\ell_{n}\sim 2\pi n r_{\mathrm{CH}}$
each contribute $(\Delta\ell_{n})^{-4}$ to $\langle\Tmn\rangle_{\mathrm{ren}}$~\cite{KimThorne1991}.
We adapt that bookkeeping to the present ergoregion setting by replacing the
hypothetical chronology radius $r_{\mathrm{CH}}$ with the ergosurface scale
$r_{\mathrm{ERGO}}$, which controls the proper circumference of equatorial
null windings near $\Sigma_{\mathrm{ERGO}}$:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:winding-sum}
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(\Delta\ell_{n})^{-4}
\;\sim\;
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(2\pi n r_{\mathrm{ERGO}})^{-4}.
\end{equation}
In a minimal-coupling idealization with vanishing coincidence scale
$\Delta\ell\to 0$, one recovers the familiar $(\Delta\ell)^{-4}$ blue-shift pole,
independently of the convergent $p$-series tail of~\eqref{eq:winding-sum}.

With the scalar-tensor cutoff~\eqref{eq:l-star-full}, the Hadamard
coincidence tube is smeared at radius $\ell_{\star}$: modes with
proper separation below $\ell_{\star}$ are suppressed by the modified
coefficient $v(x,x')$ carrying $\ln(\sigma_{\epsilon}/\mu^{2})$.
The number of resolvable windings about a circle of radius $r_{\mathrm{ERGO}}$
is therefore bounded by
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:n-max}
n_{\max}\sim \frac{2\pi r_{\mathrm{ERGO}}}{\ell_{\star}}
= 2\pi r_{\mathrm{ERGO}}
\Bigl(\frac{|R|_{\Sigma_{\mathrm{ERGO}}}}{\hbar\,|\zeta\,\phif|}\Bigr)^{\!1/2},
\end{equation}
and the regulated partial sum satisfies
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:truncated-sum}
\sum_{n=1}^{n_{\max}}(\Delta\ell_{n})^{-4}
\le \frac{1}{(2\pi r_{\mathrm{ERGO}})^{4}}
\sum_{n=1}^{n_{\max}}\frac{1}{n^{4}}
<\frac{\pi^{4}}{90\,(2\pi r_{\mathrm{ERGO}})^{4}}<\infty.
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Qualitative renormalized stress-energy estimate; analytical outlook only]
\label{prop:bounded-Tren}
Let $\phif$ satisfy~\eqref{eq:modified-kg} on the background~\eqref{eq:metric}
with $m_{\mathrm{eff}}^{2}>0$ in a neighborhood of the ergosurface $\Sigma_{\mathrm{ERGO}}$,
and assume a Hadamard parametrix of the form~\eqref{eq:hadamard} with
renormalization scale $\mu$.
Then, \emph{within the cutoff-regulated scalar-tensor modeling sector defined by}
$\ell_{\star}$, one may write the formal estimate
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:master-bound}
\bigl\|
\langle\Tmn\rangle_{\mathrm{ren}}
\bigr\|_{\infty,\Sigma_{\mathrm{ERGO}}}
\lesssim
\frac{\hbar}{4\pi^{2}\,\ell_{\star}^{4}}
=
\frac{|R|_{\Sigma_{\mathrm{ERGO}}}^{2}}{4\pi^{2}\,\hbar\,(\zeta\,\phif)^{2}}
<\infty,
\end{equation}
where $\ell_{\star}$ is given by~\eqref{eq:l-star-full}.
The null-winding sum~\eqref{eq:winding-sum} is truncated at
$n_{\max}$~\eqref{eq:n-max}, yielding the finite estimate~\eqref{eq:truncated-sum}.
This statement is a \emph{qualitative EFT outlook} adapted to an ergoregion geometry;
it is not a derived computational theorem, not a chronology-protection result, and it is
not numerically verified by the Rust monodromy or geodesic engines.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Heuristic sketch]
Substituting~\eqref{eq:hadamard} into the point-splitting formula
$\langle\Tmn\rangle_{\mathrm{ren}}=\lim_{x'\to x}
\mathcal{T}_{\mu\nu}[G^{(1)}(x,x')]$~\cite{BirrellDavies1982},
the $\sigma^{-2}$ coincidence divergence is canceled by the
$i\epsilon$ prescription and the renormalization of $v$.
The remaining $\sigma^{-1}$ contribution is modeled as regulated by
$m_{\mathrm{eff}}^{2}$ from~\eqref{eq:m-eff}: for $m_{\mathrm{eff}}^{2}>0$
the massive parametrix supplies the factor
$\exp(-m_{\mathrm{eff}}\sqrt{2\sigma})$ as $\sigma\to 0$.
Near $\Sigma_{\mathrm{ERGO}}$, the ultraviolet sensitivity of vacuum polarization
is controlled by the coincidence scale; the scalar-tensor smearing replaces
an idealized $\Delta\ell\to 0$ by $\ell_{\star}$, motivating the local
scale $\hbar/(4\pi^{2}\ell_{\star}^{4})$.
Independently, the winding sum is truncated at $n_{\max}$~\eqref{eq:n-max},
so that~\eqref{eq:truncated-sum} remains finite.
Combining the ultraviolet cutoff with the truncated winding sum
motivates~\eqref{eq:master-bound} as a qualitative EFT estimate on the ergosurface.
\end{proof}

For a static observer outside the ergoregion ($g_{tt}<0$),
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:rho-ren-bound}
\bigl|\langle\rho\rangle_{\mathrm{ren}}\bigr|
\le \hbar/(4\pi^{2}\ell_{\star}^{4}) < \infty
\end{equation}
is the corresponding formal density estimate under the same cutoff assumption.

\paragraph{Relation to background curvature scales.}
Because $\ell_{\star}$ tracks $|R|^{-1/2}$ at $\Sigma_{\mathrm{ERGO}}$, the bound
tightens in weakly curved dragging regions and loosens only when the curvature
invariant becomes large.
For the explicit profiles~\eqref{eq:profiles}, $R$ remains finite for $r>r_{0}$,
so $\ell_{\star}$ is a finite positive scale once $\zeta\,\phif\neq 0$.
This curvature linkage is the principal physical justification for preferring
$\zeta\,\phif R$ over an \emph{ad hoc} fixed cutoff: the regulator is tied to
the same geometric data that define the ergoregion, while remaining strictly
outside the certified numerical scope of criterion~(C3).
