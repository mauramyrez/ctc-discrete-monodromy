\author{Mauricio Miguel Paniagua Ramirez$^{1,*}$}

\keywords{closed timelike curves, Novikov consistency, Morris--Thorne wormholes, discrete monodromy, Banach fixed-point theorem, computational general relativity}

\begin{abstract}
We develop a reproducible computational and functional-analytic framework for verifying Novikov field self-consistency on stationary, axisymmetric Morris--Thorne geometries with azimuthal frame dragging.
Closed-form metric profiles $\{\Phir,\br,\omegar\}$ define a Lorentzian background with flare-out at the throat and a chronology-violating equatorial locus wherever $e^{2\Phir}<r^{2}\omegar^{2}$ [criterion~(C1)].
A Yukawa-type retarded kernel with mode-augmented mass $m_{\ast}$ is sampled on an $N$-node azimuthal loop and row-sum renormalized by $\exp(-m_{\ast}\Delta\tau)$ to produce a discrete monodromy operator $\MN$ on $\mathbb{R}^{N}$.
We prove that $\MN$ is a strict contraction in a discrete $H^{1}$-proxy norm whenever $m^{2}>0$, so the Banach fixed-point theorem yields a unique Novikov fixed point and geometric Picard convergence [criterion~(C3)].
A typed Rust integration engine corroborates these claims: all $17/17$ unit tests pass, with spectral radius $\|K\|_{L^{2}}<1$ and Picard residual $<10^{-8}$ in fewer than $500$ iterations for $N\in\{32,64,128\}$.
The equatorial Einstein tensor is exported symbolically (SymPy) as a background asset [criterion~(C2)]; a curvature-cutoff Hadamard bound is stated only as an effective-field-theory (EFT) outlook [criterion~(C4)], not as a numerical result.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Closed timelike curves (\ctc{}s) raise precise questions of PDE well-posedness and field self-consistency on chronologically nontrivial backgrounds~\cite{Hawking1992,Novikov1989,Friedman1990,KimThorne1991}.
The Novikov principle requires that any field configuration on a closed causal curve equal its image under transport around the loop~\cite{Novikov1989,Friedman1990}.
This paper treats that principle as a \emph{computational fixed-point problem} on an explicit frame-dragging wormhole geometry~\cite{Morris1988,Visser1995}, distinguishing carefully between continuous analytic models and the discrete numerical proxies that are actually implemented and tested.

We organize the contribution around four criteria of decreasing computational status:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(C1)] \textbf{Geometric regularity (implemented):}
    smooth profiles with flare-out and an equatorial chronology-violating locus $e^{2\Phir}<r^{2}\omegar^{2}$;
  \item[(C2)] \textbf{Symbolic background stress-energy (exported):}
    equatorial $\Gmn$ and NEC/WEC contractions obtained by SymPy as static constraints, not as dynamically evolved Rust tensors;
  \item[(C3)] \textbf{Discrete Sobolev contraction (core result):}
    unique Novikov fixed point for a mass-gap--renormalized monodromy matrix $\MN$, verified by Banach--Picard iteration in Rust;
  \item[(C4)] \textbf{Hadamard EFT outlook (analytic proposal):}
    a curvature cutoff $\ell_{\star}$ regulating the Kim--Thorne coincidence pole within a non-minimal scalar-tensor sector---not computed by the numerical engine.
\end{enumerate}

Throughout, ``continuous'' statements motivate the kernel form; all certified numerical claims refer to the discrete operator $\MN$ constructed in Sec.~\ref{sec:novikov}.
Criterion~(C3) is the primary result.
The reproducible pipeline is SymPy tensor export $\to$ typed Rust integration $\to$ IOP journal \LaTeX\ rendering (Sec.~\ref{sec:conclusion}), with all companion artifacts released in the open-source repository~\cite{PaniaguaRamirez2026CTC}.

\section{Geometry and profile class}
\label{sec:geometry}

We adopt the stationary, axisymmetric line element
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:metric}
\begin{split}
\mathrm{d}s^{2}
&= -e^{2\Phir}\,\mathrm{d}t^{2}
 + \frac{\mathrm{d}r^{2}}{1-\br/r}
 + r^{2}\,\mathrm{d}\theta^{2} \\
&\quad + r^{2}\sin^{2}\theta\,\bigl(\mathrm{d}\varphi - \omegar\,\mathrm{d}t\bigr)^{2},
\end{split}
\end{equation}
with coordinates $x^{\mu}=(t,r,\theta,\varphi)$ and
$\det(g_{\mu\nu})=-r^{5}e^{2\Phir}\sin^{2}\theta/(r-\br)$.

\subsection{Explicit metric profiles}

We specify the closed-form parameterization used throughout the Rust crate and the symbolic pipeline,
\begin{align}
\label{eq:profiles}
\br &= r_{0}\Bigl(\frac{r_{0}}{r}\Bigr)^{\gamma},
& \Phir &= -\frac{\alpha r_{0}}{r},
& \omegar &= \omega_{0}\exp\!\Bigl[-\beta\frac{r-r_{0}}{r_{0}}\Bigr],
\end{align}
with $\gamma>0$, $\alpha\ge 0$, $\omega_{0}>0$, and $\beta>0$.
At the throat $r=r_{0}$: $b(r_{0})=r_{0}$ and $b'(r_{0})=-\gamma$, so the flare-out condition $b'<1$ holds whenever $\gamma>0$~\cite{Morris1988}.

\paragraph{Reference parameters.}
Unless otherwise stated, numerical diagnostics use the crate reference profile
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:prd-params}
r_{0}=1,\quad
\gamma=0.5,\quad
\alpha=0.1,\quad
\omega_{0}=1.2,\quad
\beta=2.0,
\end{equation}
with the monodromy and geodesic loops evaluated at $r_{\mathrm{loop}}=1.5\,r_{0}$.

\paragraph{Admissibility bounds (C1).}
\begin{align}
\label{eq:bounds}
\gamma &> 0
&& \text{(flare-out)}, \nonumber\\
e^{-2\alpha} &< r_{0}^{2}\omega_{0}^{2}
&& \text{(CTC opens at throat)}, \nonumber\\
r_{\mathrm{CTC}} &> r_{0}
&& \text{(chronology-violating locus exterior to throat)}.
\end{align}
The onset radius $r_{\mathrm{CTC}}$ is the smallest $r>r_{0}$ solving $e^{2\Phir(r)}=r^{2}\omegar(r)^{2}$, computed in the crate by bisection.

\subsection{Frame dragging, chronology horizon, and CTC locus}

The Killing combination $\xi=\partial_{t}+\Omega\partial_{\varphi}$ is timelike when
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ctc-criterion}
-e^{2\Phir}+2\Omega\,\omegar\, r^{2}\sin^{2}\theta
+\Omega^{2}r^{2}\sin^{2}\theta<0.
\end{equation}
On the equatorial plane ($\theta=\pi/2$) with $\Omega=\omegar$, this reduces to
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:ctc-simple}
e^{2\Phir} < r^{2}\omegar^{2}.
\end{equation}
We write $\Sigma_{\mathrm{ERGO}}$ for the surface $e^{2\Phir}=r^{2}\omegar^{2}$: it is the equatorial frame-dragging boundary at which $-\partial_{t}$ becomes null, and the chronology-violating locus for co-rotating observers opens wherever~\eqref{eq:ctc-simple} holds (the chronology horizon in the sense of the present stationary geometry).
Null slopes in the $(t,\varphi)$ subspace are
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:null-slopes}
\Bigl(\frac{\mathrm{d}\varphi}{\mathrm{d}t}\Bigr)_{\!\pm}=\omegar\pm e^{\Phir}/r,
\end{equation}
so the future light cone encompasses $-\partial_{t}$ when~\eqref{eq:ctc-simple} holds (Fig.~\ref{fig:lightcones}).

Christoffel symbols used for geodesic diagnostics are obtained from~\eqref{eq:metric} by central finite differences with step $\varepsilon=10^{-5}$.
Closed timelike initial data at $r_{\mathrm{loop}}$ are normalized so that $g(u,u)=-1$ with $u\propto(1,0,0,\omega)$, and integrated by classical RK4 with step $\Delta\tau=0.01$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:geodesic}).

\section{Symbolic Einstein tensor and energy conditions}
\label{sec:energy}

Einstein's equations read $\Gmn=\Rmn-\tfrac12 R\,g_{\mu\nu}=8\pi\Tmn$.
The equatorial components of $\Gmn$ and the associated NEC/WEC contractions are obtained by symbolic differentiation in SymPy (Appendices~\ref{app:tensors}--\ref{app:energy}) and treated as \emph{background constraints} on the profile class~\eqref{eq:profiles}.
They are not assembled dynamically from curvature inside the Rust integration engine; the crate's energy-condition unit tests exercise only scaffold quadratic forms for API sanity checks.

\section{Dynamic stability: discrete Sobolev contraction}
\label{sec:novikov}

Novikov's principle requires that fields on a closed causal curve equal their image under transport around the loop.
We motivate the transport map from the massive Klein--Gordon equation on~\eqref{eq:metric},
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:kg}
\frac{1}{\sqrt{-g}}\,\partial_{\mu}\!\Bigl(\sqrt{-g}\,g^{\mu\nu}\partial_{\nu}\phi\Bigr)-m^{2}\phi=0,
\end{equation}
but we do \emph{not} discretize the full curved-space PDE.
Instead we construct an explicit discrete monodromy operator on the azimuthal loop that inherits a Yukawa mass gap from~\eqref{eq:kg} and is contractive by design.

\subsection{Continuous Yukawa proxy}

Decomposing into azimuthal modes $\phi\sim\psi_{\nu}(r)\,e^{i(\nu\varphi-\Omega t)}$ suggests a mode-augmented mass
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:m-star}
m_{\ast}^{2}=m^{2}+\nu^{2}/g_{\varphi\varphi},
\qquad
G(\Delta s)\sim\frac{m_{\ast}}{4\pi\Delta s}\,e^{-m_{\ast}\Delta s},
\end{equation}
for a retarded Yukawa-type kernel along a closed timelike trajectory $\gamma(\tau)$.
Equation~\eqref{eq:m-star} is the continuous \emph{proxy} that motivates the discrete construction below; it is not claimed to be the exact retarded Green function of~\eqref{eq:kg} on~\eqref{eq:metric}.

\subsection{Discrete monodromy operator}

\begin{definition}[Discrete monodromy $\MN$]
\label{def:MN}
Fix $r=r_{\mathrm{loop}}$, $\theta=\pi/2$, and $N$ uniform nodes $\chi_{i}=2\pi i/N$ along the azimuthal circle.
Let $\Delta\varphi=2\pi/N$ and $\Delta\tau=\sqrt{g_{\varphi\varphi}}\,\Delta\varphi$.
For forward proper separations $\Delta s_{j\to i}$ along the loop, define the sampled kernel matrix
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:K-raw}
\widetilde{K}_{ij}
=
\begin{cases}
G(\Delta s_{j\to i})\,\Delta\tau, & i\neq j,\\
0, & i=j,
\end{cases}
\end{equation}
with $G$ from~\eqref{eq:m-star}.
The discrete monodromy operator is the \emph{row-sum renormalized} matrix
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:K-renorm}
K_{ij}
=
\widetilde{K}_{ij}\,
\frac{\exp(-m_{\ast}\Delta\tau)}{\sum_{k}\widetilde{K}_{ik}}
\qquad\bigl(\text{when the row sum is nonzero}\bigr),
\end{equation}
and $\MN:\mathbb{R}^{N}\to\mathbb{R}^{N}$ acts by $\Phi\mapsto K\Phi$.
\end{definition}

Row renormalization forces every row of $K$ to sum to $\exp(-m_{\ast}\Delta\tau)<1$ whenever $m_{\ast}>0$.
Consequently the spectral radius satisfies $\rho(K)\le\exp(-m_{\ast}\Delta\tau)<1$ for this constructed operator.
The default crate parameters are $r_{\mathrm{loop}}=1.5\,r_{0}$, $m^{2}=1$, $\nu=1$, and $N=128$, with tolerances $\mathrm{tol}=10^{-12}$ (solver) and residual thresholds $10^{-8}$--$10^{-10}$ (tests).

\subsection{Discrete $H^{1}$-proxy norm and contraction}

On periodic samples $\Phi\in\mathbb{R}^{N}$ we use the discrete norms
\begin{align}
\label{eq:discrete-norms}
\|\Phi\|_{L^{2}}^{2}
&=
\frac1N\sum_{i=0}^{N-1}\Phi_{i}^{2},
\qquad
\|\Phi\|_{H^{1}}^{2}
=
\|\Phi\|_{L^{2}}^{2}
+
\|\delta\Phi\|_{L^{2}}^{2},
\end{align}
where $(\delta\Phi)_{i}=(\Phi_{i+1}-\Phi_{i-1})/2$ is the central difference on the index circle (the crate's $H^{1}$-proxy; not a continuum Sobolev norm on $\gamma$).
The $L^{2}$ operator norm of $K$ is estimated by power iteration; the discrete Sobolev lift used for the Lipschitz bound is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:discrete-sobolev}
L
\le
\|K\|_{L^{2}}\,
\Bigl(1+\frac{\pi\sqrt{2}}{N}\Bigr).
\end{equation}

\begin{lemma}[Discrete Sobolev contraction]
\label{lem:sobolev}
Assume $m^{2}>0$ and the background geometry is bounded on the loop at $r_{\mathrm{loop}}$.
Let $K$ be the renormalized matrix~\eqref{eq:K-renorm}.
If $\|K\|_{L^{2}}<1$ and the proxy bound~\eqref{eq:discrete-sobolev} satisfies $L<1$, then $\MN$ is a strict contraction on $(\mathbb{R}^{N},\|\cdot\|_{H^{1}})$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By construction each row of $K$ sums to $\exp(-m_{\ast}\Delta\tau)<1$, so $\rho(K)<1$ and the power-iteration estimate of $\|K\|_{L^{2}}$ is strictly less than unity for the reference parameters.
The discrete lift~\eqref{eq:discrete-sobolev} then yields $L<1$, hence
\[
\|\MN\Phi-\MN\Psi\|_{H^{1}}
\le
L\,\|\Phi-\Psi\|_{H^{1}}
\]
for all $\Phi,\Psi\in\mathbb{R}^{N}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Novikov consistency for $\MN$]
\label{thm:novikov-stability}
Under Lemma~\ref{lem:sobolev}, there exists a unique fixed point $\Phi^{*}=K\Phi^{*}$ in $\mathbb{R}^{N}$, and the Picard iteration $\Phi_{k+1}=K\Phi_{k}$ converges geometrically from arbitrary initial data in $\mathbb{R}^{N}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Lemma~\ref{lem:sobolev} places $\MN$ as a strict contraction on the Banach space $(\mathbb{R}^{N},\|\cdot\|_{H^{1}})$.
The Banach fixed-point theorem yields existence and uniqueness of $\Phi^{*}$, together with geometric convergence of Picard iterates.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Numerical verification (C3).}
\label{sec:numerical}
The CTC Research Framework implements Definition~\ref{def:MN} and Theorem~\ref{thm:novikov-stability} in Rust.
All $17/17$ unit tests pass, covering:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(i)] geometric/\ctc{} metric invariants and flare-out for~\eqref{eq:prd-params} (5);
  \item[(ii)] scaffold energy-condition API diagnostics (2);
  \item[(iii)] monodromy bounds $\|K\|_{L^{2}}<1$ and $L<1$ (3);
  \item[(iv)] Banach--Picard convergence $\|\Phi^{*}-K\Phi^{*}\|_{2}<10^{-8}$ in fewer than $500$ iterations for $N\in\{32,64,128\}$ (5);
  \item[(v)] RK4 integration of CTC-normalized geodesics with $\Delta\tau=0.01$ over $200$ steps (2).
\end{enumerate}
These tests corroborate the discrete contraction theory for $\MN$; they do not certify that the unnormalized continuum Green function of~\eqref{eq:kg} is itself an $H^{1}(\gamma)$ contraction.

\section{Semiclassical Hadamard bound: EFT outlook}
\label{sec:backreaction}

Semiclassical analyses predict a $(\Delta\ell)^{-4}$ divergence of $\langle\Tmn\rangle_{\mathrm{ren}}$ as null geodesics accumulate on the chronology horizon~\cite{KimThorne1991,Hawking1992}.

\paragraph{Comparison with the Kay--Radzikowski--Wald no-go theorem.}
\label{sec:kay-wald}
Kay, Radzikowski, and Wald~\cite{KayRadzikowskiWald1997} proved that no Hadamard state exists throughout a neighborhood of a compactly generated Cauchy horizon under \emph{minimal} coupling.
Proposition~\ref{prop:bounded-Tren} does not contradict that result: Kay--Wald assumes $\zeta=0$ and a globally Hadamard state, whereas $\zeta\,\phif R$ supplies the curvature cutoff $\ell_{\star}$, modifies the short-distance parametrix, and truncates null windings at $n_{\max}\sim 2\pi r_{\mathrm{CH}}/\ell_{\star}$.
The bound is cutoff-regulated within an EFT sector; it is not a claim that a minimally coupled Hadamard state extends across $\Sigma_{\mathrm{ERGO}}$, nor that the numerical engine computes $\langle\Tmn\rangle_{\mathrm{ren}}$.

\section{Conclusion and methodological contribution}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have constructed a reproducible computational pipeline---symbolic GR export (SymPy), typed integration (Rust), and document rendering (IOP journal \LaTeX)---for Novikov consistency verification on axisymmetric frame-dragging wormhole backgrounds.
The complete companion codebase, including the discrete monodromy engine, SymPy exports, unit tests, and manuscript sources, is publicly available at
\url{https://github.com/mauramyrez/ctc-discrete-monodromy}~\cite{PaniaguaRamirez2026CTC}
(manuscript date: \manuscriptdate).
The single, defensible contribution is:

\begin{quote}
\emph{We construct an explicit frame-dragging Morris--Thorne profile class, export its equatorial Einstein tensor symbolically, and demonstrate that a mass-gap--renormalized discrete monodromy operator is an $H^{1}$-proxy contraction with a unique Picard fixed point.}
\end{quote}

In terms of the criteria~(C1)--(C4):
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{(C1)} Explicit profiles~\eqref{eq:profiles} with flare-out, reference parameters~\eqref{eq:prd-params}, and equatorial chronology-violating locus~\eqref{eq:ctc-simple}, corroborated by metric and RK4 diagnostics.
  \item \textbf{(C2)} SymPy-exported equatorial $\Gmn$ and NEC/WEC contractions as background constraints (Appendices~\ref{app:tensors}--\ref{app:energy}), not dynamically generated in Rust.
  \item \textbf{(C3)} Unique Novikov fixed point for the renormalized discrete operator $\MN$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:novikov-stability}), with $17/17$ Rust tests, $\|K\|_{L^{2}}<1$, and Picard residual $<10^{-8}$ in $<500$ iterations for $N\in\{32,64,128\}$.
  \item \textbf{(C4)} Analytic EFT proposal for a curvature-cutoff Hadamard bound (Proposition~\ref{prop:bounded-Tren}); not numerically computed.
\end{itemize}

Global hyperbolicity is not restored once chronology-violating loci exist; Cauchy-problem statements remain local to chronology-respecting regions.
Observational constraints and a continuum limit of $\MN$ without row renormalization are deferred to future work.
